Skidding into shutdown

     It's really difficult to write about this.  I'm in an empty space, a slow skid to a lower gear, adjusting and calibrating my moods, as massive demands of work throttle back, thin out, obligations are met, and the pervasive assaults by administors diminish.
     Anyone who denies me is an arrogant ass.  We're asked to do a gargantuan amount of family support, interviews, testing, report writing, meetings, and that has fuqall to do with class prep, assessing what kids need, then to teach, encourage, intervene, grade, reteach.  Next! analyze fake data with your team.  It's two jobs in one.  But that's not enough.  Turns out, you're doing it wrong.  Mandated professional development has been selected regardless of your opinion, so you must be doing it wrong.  You will do the PD.  Administrators pop in unannounced and do endless, fucking endless observations, whispering to your students, taking iPad notes, clacking away in their high heels with a twinkling finger wave, never to report anything back to you.  You don't learn from it.  There's nothing to learn.
     Wait, there's one important lesson:  you are meat.  You don't matter.
