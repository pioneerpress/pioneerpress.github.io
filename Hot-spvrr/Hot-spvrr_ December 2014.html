Wednesday, December 31, 2014

New Old Year's

     This is the sunset on 12-31-2014.  Goodbye, 2014.  I'm not sorry to see you go.  Don't let the earth's frozen crust smack you on the way out.  It's been really hard emotionally, "stressfully," financially, health-wise, career-wise, weatherwise, outlets keep shorting out, the garbage disposal doesn't work, and the windows rattle in the wind.  The kind of hard that makes one contemplate -- if one contemplates anything amid the noise of daily living, and doesn't just stand stock still and groan.  My home has been a front line of struggle, and not easy, even to allude to it.  Karen and I have our love of one another and tons of common interests, and we'll be all right.  Sam has his struggles, the steep incline to find his place under two household's roofs, when no employer is hiring for his multiple skills, and not one skill quite exactly right.  And so on he slogs at minimum wage with maximum student debt, which is half ours.  Juju strives to stay afloat with six pre-IB courses, and is learning new ways to learn, to maximize her time.  With adhd, she has speed of functioning difficulties, which push obligations all over everywhere, as she's always interested in what's going on over there.  Thank God for her incredible reading ability, vocabulary, math intuition, her appreciation of diversity, and ferocious devotion to fairness.  She is ferocious!  Her toddler self would shout, "I already know dat!"
     Anyway, it's hard to look anywhere but one's own nearest proximity during times like these.  Who can look up, take that 1,000 yard stare?  Who can see the sun?  And every time I do, like right above here, I notice it's sinking before I've captured it, and the child inside me wants to reach out and hold onto it.  Because, what'll I do when the sun goes down?
     And like Juju, I'm out of time, and have to deal, to delegate, to move.  I haven't done justice to the changes the year has wrought, wrung out, sprinkled down upon us.  They'll have to materialize in my next sitdown.  Food is waiting to be served, New Year's Eve is waiting, real life is calling.  The cusp of the next big thing is calling.  And I can't report until I see it.  Ciao4niao, love and peace.  hotspvrr.

Saturday, December 27, 2014

Coming out of the blue

     I don't have anything up my sleeves, but I'm coming at this out of the blue, and the more I write, the more I find up my sleeve, stuck in my teeth, clogged in my arteries, blasting out the brine.
     Out of the blue a jet careens, screaming, contrails tumbling in purplewhite braids.
     This is where I'm at today, and it needs to be fucking said.  I'm so tired of happy talk, and pussy footing around gobs of clogged whimsy and metaphor.
     I'm angry about what's been done to my friend, a teacher who had no advocates, no corner man, no wing woman, no help for his torment.  
     I'm thinking of one of my good friends, one whose (paycheck) career has come to a close, shredded by the district, eaten out by the powers who dismantle, denude, and unravel souls.  The ones who take you out behind the shed, and basically kill your magic with kids.  You can be an American with a Disability; you may have taught, tenured, for 23 or 46 or 3 years; I know from experience that it doesn't matter if you've suffered from a spinal injury on the job; it doesn't matter if you've been bounced from school to different age groups to different curricular adoptions; and if your nerves have exacerbated your ADHD to the point of mouldered executive function....  God.   They can, in their infinite wisdom, snatch and jerk your classroom keys away year after year, at mid-year, change your kids and curriculum, your comfort zone of knowledge, itself a good zone, one gained by practice and tutelage, basically, hard. ass. work.  They tell you you'll get help, not judgment or punishment.  Help is on the way in the form of a Peer!  But up above or down below in the principal's vestibule, they can consult without you... decide to take you outside to the stocks, to the pillory, they can throw tomatoes of new positions back into your face, and like any good secret police, they can throw up their hands and scream, "What the fuck doesn't he understand?"  "There's nothing left for us to do but abandon him."  It's a screaming tragedy, a tragedy I can't help or touch with my 10 fingered blog entry spitting out epithets.

Tuesday, December 23, 2014

Talking off a big pain in the neck!

     This is the longest I've left my blog, I think, in the year since I began it.  What, four days?  So right now, I'm a little scared of my laziness, my flatness regarding anything fresh to update.  
     So I'm back on.
     I almost putzed my way around restarting it right here, too.  Can't do that.
     Karen's lying down; Juju's in her room, wrapping a present for me; Sam's actually home, upstairs -- kind of the four corners of the bedspread, flattened out.
     So.
     So what?
     Maybe I'll let this be journalish in nature.  Personable-journalish.  
     I got test results back from an MRI this morning confirming that I do have reason for the pain in my neck!  Waiting to hear what the neurosurgery department says in response, but I've got no padding, lots of rawness, erosion.  Some of the hardest to understand English I've ever seen!
"C5-C6: Moderate intervertebral disc height and T2 signal loss is 
demonstrated, with grade 1 retrolisthesis of C5 with respect to C6. 
Posterior disc osteophyte complex effaces the ventral thecal sac without 
contacting the ventral spinal cord...."
     I think what it's saying is that I have a pain in my neck!  
     Words are weird.  Earlier today I said one word aloud (forgot which one -- it might've been an odd /th/ word, like kinesthesis, or isthmus, or psithurism), then wondered if Juju heard me.  I was working on the bathtub grout.  It was like a mushroom, this word, the only visible portion of the organism in my meadow of a head.  The adhd so-o, so-o needs to relax after prolonged alertness.  To me, words... when ground into a paste with a mortar and pestle, then wetted in a roux with flour, water, and salt, words quiet the ions in the air, alert your mouth, turn you wizardly.  You really can mouth prayers, chant spells, shout epithets, murmur niceties.  Like ball lightning, language.
     Words are weird, though.  What came before that word in your mouth?  Anything?  Nothing?  What prompted it?  Was there just nothing before you spoke?  I've always been curious about the instant a word forms, the processes that gave rise to its formation, but I doubt I'd have the learned curiosity to pursue the study of speech.  Well, anything can prompt it, but what was there but emptiness beforehand?  I know the emptiness before words.  I have this emptiness all the time.  Pop, I talk.  I don't get the materialization of speech.  The fact I stand so archly opposed to speaking to someone when I don't feel like it, that then I can suddenly blurt/utter syllables, anything/something despite my black resistance, to the cashier, to the shopping cart jockey in the parking lot, says something weird about the substance of language.  I guess for lack of anything else, I think it's kind of spiritual.
     I just go from a hidden black well in me (do you? do people have this?) a hidden black well that returns a little pail with a little ladle on a frayed cord, and inside that little pail are liquid unformed thoughts.  Eventually, if I don't use the liquid rising in this pail, which is slowly replenishing itself, it drips over its edges, so it has to be used.  I think, without ridding myself of the ideas, the overflowing pail makes me crazy.  So then it's unavoidable; I have to express something.
     Part of what kept me from typing on this little black iPad keyboard was the fact I needed to mend my soul from the blizzard of work, to let things go a bit.  But when I've had the time, I've felt growing aches in my neck and shoulders as days wore into afternoons, the pain sometimes arriving as early as the morning and intensifying all day from this knot in the left-hand base of my cervical spine.  I've known it's been injured from my Linwood days in 2001 when dancing like a rag doll, all herky-jerky, at a gathering for my literary arts students, who then put on a poetry slam.  Chiropractic and PT got me through another two years, and then surgery was required, and it helped, but they allowed that two vertebrae still needed eventual attention.  Now the buzzing, numb left index finger gives it away, and I'll probably have to have surgery.  We'll see!  
     Anyway, words come, they very imperceptibly arise, and pop!  Have to be said.  I have more on this, but it'll come later.  I don't like it when I go all expository, newsy.  That's too much prostitution.
     It does help the psychic pressure to grab this handle and ladle off some of the words.  Funny, as a supply, they're infinitely replenishing.  Just so damn slow!  And so hard to control!

Tuesday, December 16, 2014

Why?

     Why do I write such drippy schmaltzy stuff?  I don't know.  I never explain myself.  I ought to at least explain myself sometimes, anchor the moorings. Say where it came from, where it was headed, why it morphed into something else where the one phrase that inspired it got shredded to the floor.  I don't know.  I should at least explain myself sometimes.  Okay so, the last poem came from a line that isn't in it anymore. "It's all wound down / to nothing."  Which, the more I looked at it, the more I left it behind.  Anyway, the rest of it became sort of a mulling at first, then a series of verbal actions towards the end.  I don't think it warranted a second look.  But my stuff never garners a first look. I don't know where that expression came from any more than a musician plays four notes and likes them and makes up a song out of them.  So that was that, and then it became a riff on fog. Like, immediately. Just, lots of inert pictures, pictures of things that relaxed in the fog we're experiencing this second week of December.  Now the cold and wind is returning to the darkest week of the year. So, I had to wrap it up before the mood changed to something more hoary and horrible. Okay.  Gotta sleep.

is winding down
coughed out
exhaled away 
to nowhere. 
quicksilver spraypaint
atomized black fenders
a boiled wool coat
lapel up, back turned
to the damp
windows shuttered
newspapers rolled
in rubber bands
dead in water.
sparrows perched 
in different directions
the crackling leaf pile
now flat, steamed  
tan tree corpses
and other boughs 
drape loving arms 
round a memory, 
back behind the door
edges stirred and blurred
the watch fob is loose
couch springs sprung
the gate finally jammed
the muffler fallen off
pebbles in the alley
rolled to the bottom
mess everywhere
evenly spread

Thursday, December 11, 2014

Loved his love of music

     There goes my hand to turn up the volume, and I remember how Johnny turned up the volume.  I remember Johnny's love of music.  He and I were arch rivals, didn't think anywhere near alike -- oh, we snapped and horse-laughed at the same idiocies -- but I felt riveted by his sort of rapture with tuneage.  He was a big Baby-Huey-in-a-bathtub-fully-clothed when he liked a song that came on.  Kind of senseless, was my impression.  Mozart senseless.  Elvis senseless.  Hank senseless.  Jeff Buckley senseless in the senseless way slipstreamers slide through time without counting up or down beats.  
     I go back, no -- I rocket back in time to the mid 70s in his car, maybe a Dodge Omni, with a portable reel-to-reel cassette blaster on the front seat between us.  When he liked a Taj Mahal tune, Weather Report, or a Shostakovich String Quartet, he'd say, "It's coming!" and he'd torque up the volume!  Which was odd for someone so sensitive to sound.  Kind of the Brian Wilson of the family.  And my chin would tuck back:  Louder?  My eardrums thrummed, like a beltway of bathing choruses.  Like Stravinsky on that film footage with a cigarette fist conducting one handed, Johnny would, one hand on the wheel, the other like hawk talons, clawing the dashboard plastic.  ba, ba, bA, BA!
     That gorgeous, senseless slide along the slipstream.  To be sense-less.  Just a little while, less than a block in traffic, we could jump a wormhole.
     In this way, we could be higher, a little extra, totally alert, but feeling a cool breeze over our foreheads.  
     I'll never hold the sense of it.  The sense of it.  I can talk all night about senselessness, about our favorites, those Dylan or Bix Beiderbecke or Keith Jarrett or Wayne Shorter pieces that shut out everyone, leave everything behind, shut us up, gobsmack the nonsense in the white daylight.
     No, I'll never grasp it.  I might have it in here, behind my upper teeth, on the tip of my hard palate where the tongue feels strange lunar edges.  But I'll never pick up this chalice, this senseless feeling, take it in my hands, and be able to show it to anyone.  I don't know how.
     I don't know how.

Early Saturday night to a teacher

     As he grades away the copper winter afternoon, the sun drowning in kerosene swirled in cold robin's egg blue over a pee-colored snow field of Cretin Derham, he keeps checking, writing, plugging in numbers to a shaky website, and the outdoors goes dim... dunkel.  Look up, look down, eyes in here, and out there... the black windows... all dims, all dims.  Damn.  Damn dimming!  [his own raging against the dying of the light].  Nothing to greet the rising orange moon and stupid orange sherbet streetlights.

How peers love their peers

     In a forest of big legs, the baby boy in the stroller locks eyes on the toddler wobbling towards him through a grove of six footers.  His eyes follow that little boy.  They're kin.  In that way, so do old folks in the lobby of the home, facing each other, griping, unless a toddler toddles by.  We all ride along a rough rusted track, those of us in our mid-fifties, those in their young forties, late teens, all the young first grade dudes!  They're alums.  Lookin' back at kindergarten.  We are alums.  I remember seventh grade before Nixon resigned and the hatred my elders felt toward our president was hot!  There was this prevalent feeling of heat that they had to throw his ass out whether Saigon fell to Communists or not.  What the hell?  Get me back on track!  Goodness.  I always sense these rusty tracks, these cracked plastic seats on an old bus, bouncing me along, making me more claustrophobic, itchy and bitchy, kinda aware of our same-aged peers, our kin, and that just leaves me jutting my jaw.
